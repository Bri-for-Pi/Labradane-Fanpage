    • Training

    more training
    The first thing most people want to know is how they can be trained.




    Labradanes are fairly easy to train, they don't need as much time as other dogs do.

    I do reccomend training them as puppies, they are much easier then.
    Unless you want to train a 180 pound dog, then go right ahead.

    Another thing, use positive reinforcement methods while training, and avoid from any harmful or useless methods that could possibly ruin their love for you.
    Since these sweet gentle giants become very upset from just a slight bit of yelling.

    Guard Dog

    If you want to add this pup to your household as a guard dog, then it's best for you to pick another dog.

    Considering this is one of the worst guard animals ever.

    It may be a bit of a surprise because of their large size. They are huge sweethearts that couldn't hurt a fly, well not on purpose at least.

    They are good watch dogs though; the Labradane will bark in order to alert you when an intruder is near or if something's not right.
    But it's completely up to you to act, don't expect these cuties to do more than alerting.

    How Big they'll get

    Because both the parents are big dogs, the pup will be pretty large too.

    Labradanes average weight is going to be around 100-190 lbs.

    While it's height is going to be around 25-35 in tall.

    You'll more than likely have to keep buying bigger and bigger beds even as a puppy, expect a big pup.





    Behavior

    Both the Labrador and Great dane are gentle giants, huge sweethearts.
    They love receiving affection and giving love to it's owners,
    Both dogs adore cuddling and spending time with it's owner. They are perfect family dogs, but for older kids.
    The pupper could accidently smother the smaller child because of it's huge size, and it doesn't realize how big they actually are.

    As a puppy, they'll be very curious
    Since this puppies parents are one of the most affectionate breeds, I suggest this;
    Do not add this pup to your family if you don't have time to spend with it on a daily basis.

    If you don't spend enough time with it, the poor pup will develop seperation anxiety and won't live a happy lifetime.





    Good Living Conditions

    First things first, DO NOT get this dog if you're wanting to keep it inside a small area like an apartment.
    They won't be comfortable living in a small spaced house or apartment.

    A large backyard is the best thing for this giant dog

    For the weather, Labradane pups prefer moderate temperatures rather than extreme ones.
    Mostly because of it's short coats





    Proper Care

    You want to first look at how much the dog sheds, and thankfully, it's low.

    You'll only have to worry about it's coat shedding a bit above average during Shedding Season
    Brushing this pup's coat about 1-2 times a week should keep it's coat nice and tidy.

    Bathing time is generally once every 2 weeks, but if the pupper really needs it, once a week.

    As for other grooming needs;

    Once every other day is the perfect time to brush their teeth,

    clean their ears around once every week,

    and trim nails whenever it grows longer than needed.
